Refine the Attendee Welcome Flow UI and Enable Direct Meeting Scheduling on the Sponsors Step

The attendee flow is moving in the right direction, but the current implementation still needs refinement in presentation, layout, and sponsor-step interaction design.

There are three specific corrections needed:

The first page should feel like a registration confirmation / next steps page, not just a narrow survey form.

The onboarding card container is too narrow and should be widened.

On the Sponsors step, attendees should be able to schedule meetings directly from that step, including Onsite and Online options where supported.

This work is primarily UI/UX refinement, not a change to the overall flow architecture.

1. Primary Goal

Refine the attendee welcome flow so that:

Card 1 feels like a polished registration confirmation experience

the onboarding container is wider and more professional

the sponsor step supports direct meeting scheduling actions

the overall flow feels like a guided event preparation assistant

2. Card 1 Should Feel Like a Registration Confirmation Page

The first screen currently feels too much like a plain form.

We want it to feel like:

Registration Confirmed
Now let’s prepare for the conference.

This is important because the attendee has just registered and should feel that Concierge is the natural next step.

3. Add Registration Confirmation Header Treatment

At the top of Card 1, add a more polished event confirmation section.

Required elements

event logo

event name

animated / pulsing Registration Confirmed badge or graphic

short supportive message

Example content
Registration Confirmed
You’re all set for The 2026 Fintech Risk & Compliance Forum.

Now let’s personalize your event experience in about 60 seconds.

This should visually feel like a continuation of the registration journey.

4. Add “Next Steps” Framing to Card 1

Before the topic chips, add a short “Next Steps” section or micro-copy.

Example
Next Steps

1. Tell us what topics interest you
2. We’ll show you recommended sessions
3. We’ll recommend sponsors worth meeting
4. We’ll send you your personalized event plan

This does not need to be overly large, but it should frame the flow clearly.

The attendee should understand where they are headed.

5. Improve Card 1 Headline and Supporting Copy

The current copy is functional but can be improved.

Recommended structure

Headline:

What topics interest you?

Supporting copy:

Select the topics most relevant to you so Concierge can personalize your sessions, sponsors, and meeting suggestions.

Keep this attendee-friendly and polished.

6. Widen the Onboarding Container

Yes, the current onboarding container is too narrow.

It feels like an auth form width rather than a recommendation/preparation experience.

Required behavior

Increase the max width of the onboarding card/container.

Recommended target

Use something closer to:

max-width: 760px
to

max-width: 900px

depending on the current design system

The goal is to allow:

topic chips to breathe

sponsor/session cards to look more polished

content to feel less cramped

Do not make it excessively wide, but it should clearly be wider than the current narrow form layout.

7. Improve Internal Spacing and Layout

Once the card width is increased, also refine spacing:

more generous horizontal padding

cleaner spacing between header, copy, and chip groups

more room for session and sponsor cards

footer/buttons should not feel compressed

This should feel like a polished event experience, not a utility form.

8. Keep the Flow Centered and Mobile-Friendly

Even though the card should be wider on desktop, preserve a clean centered layout.

Required behavior

centered container on desktop

stacked, readable layout on tablet/mobile

topic chips wrap well on smaller screens

CTAs remain easy to tap on mobile

This should not become a desktop-only redesign.

9. Refine the Progress Step Labels

The current step labels can be improved for clarity.

Recommended labels

Instead of vague/internal labels, use attendee-friendly wording such as:

Interests

Your Email

Sessions

Sponsors

Complete

This will make the flow easier to understand.

If these labels are already close, refine them rather than rebuilding the component.

10. Sponsors Step Should Support Direct Meeting Scheduling

On the sponsor step, attendees should not only be able to “Request Meeting.”

They should be able to schedule a meeting directly where the sponsor supports it.

Required behavior

For each sponsor card on the Sponsors step, show meeting actions based on sponsor capabilities.

If sponsor supports onsite meetings

Show:

Schedule Onsite Meeting
If sponsor supports online meetings

Show:

Schedule Online Meeting
Also keep
Request Information

Do not force attendees to leave this screen just to take the next action.

This is a high-intent moment and should convert directly into meetings.

11. Use Existing Sponsor Meeting Capabilities

Do not invent new meeting types.

Use the sponsor meeting capabilities already supported in the platform, such as:

onsite meeting allowed

online meeting allowed

If a sponsor only supports one type, show only that option.

If a sponsor supports both, show both.

If a sponsor supports neither direct scheduling mode, fall back to:

Request Meeting

if that is still the correct existing behavior.

12. Sponsor Card CTA Hierarchy

The sponsor cards on the Sponsors step should have a clearer action hierarchy.

Recommended priority

Primary CTA:

Schedule Onsite Meeting
or

Schedule Online Meeting
or

Request Meeting

Secondary CTA:

Request Information

If both onsite and online scheduling are supported, show both clearly without cluttering the card.

Make sure the visual hierarchy guides attendees toward scheduling.

13. Preserve Existing Meeting / Info Request State Logic

If the attendee has already:

requested a meeting

confirmed a meeting

requested information

the card should continue to show the proper interaction state.

Examples:

Meeting Requested

Meeting Confirmed

Info Sent

Do not regress this behavior while changing the CTA layout.

14. Add Relevance Context to Sponsor Cards If Available

Where topic overlap exists, improve the sponsor cards with relevance text.

Example:

Relevant to your interests:
AML Monitoring, Fraud Defense

This is not strictly required to complete the scheduling behavior, but it should be displayed where the data already exists.

This makes the page feel much more intelligent.

15. Make the Sponsors Step Feel Like “Sponsors to Meet”

The sponsor step should feel contextual, not like a sponsor directory.

Update heading/copy as needed

Example headline:

Sponsors You May Want to Meet

Supporting copy:

These sponsors align with your interests and are available for meetings during the event.

This framing is stronger than a generic sponsor listing.

16. Preserve the 5-Card Flow

Do not change the overall flow structure.

It should remain:

Interests

Your Email / Match Preview

Suggested Sessions

Sponsors to Meet

Complete

This task is about refining how those cards look and behave.

17. Acceptance Criteria

This refinement is complete when:

Card 1 visually feels like a registration confirmation / next steps page

Card 1 includes a polished Registration Confirmed treatment

Card 1 includes clearer next-step framing

the onboarding card/container is noticeably wider and less cramped

the layout remains centered and mobile-friendly

the Sponsors step supports direct meeting scheduling actions where available

sponsors can show Onsite and/or Online meeting actions directly on the sponsor cards

Request Information remains available

existing interaction states still display correctly

18. Success Standard

After this update, the attendee onboarding flow should feel polished, intentional, and easy to understand. The first screen should feel like a continuation of registration, and the Sponsors step should make it easy for attendees to schedule meetings immediately when they are most engaged.
