Redesign Event Scheduling Page to Match Welcome Page Structure and Improve Engagement

The Event Scheduling Page should visually and structurally match the Welcome Page experience.

Many users will arrive directly from:

email links

calendar invites

sponsor outreach

meeting request emails

Therefore the page should orient the attendee quickly, explain the value of interacting with sponsors, and encourage topic selection and meeting requests.

1. Page Header Structure

Update the top section so it matches the Welcome Page layout.

Structure:

[Event Logo]

[Badge: Meeting Scheduling]

Browse Sponsors and Schedule Meetings for  
The 2026 Fintech Risk & Compliance Forum

Add a short orientation line:

Select topics you're interested in to discover sponsors aligned with your priorities.
You can request meetings, schedule online sessions, or ask for more information.

This helps users who arrive without seeing the Welcome page first.

2. Replace Current Intro Text

Remove the current text:

Browse and book a meeting with participating sponsors.
Filter by topic to find sponsors aligned with your interests.

Replace with:

Suggested replacement text:

Explore sponsors participating in the conference and connect with those aligned with your interests.

Select topics below to highlight relevant sponsors and schedule a conversation before or during the event.
3. Topic Selection Section

Make this section visually match the Welcome Page topic section.

Header:

NEXT STEP
Make the most of your time at the conference. What topics interest you?

Directly below:

Display the topic pills:

AI
Compliance
Customer Service
Legal
Regulatory

Styling:

same pill style as welcome page

clickable filter

selected state highlighted

4. Remove Extra Instruction Text

Remove this line:

Meeting formats, durations, and locations vary by sponsor and event setup.

This information is unnecessary and adds clutter.

5. Sponsor Section Header

Update section header from:

Sponsors Available for Meetings

to:

Meet With Our Sponsors

Add subtext below:

Choose a sponsor below to schedule a meeting or request more information.
6. Improve Engagement Copy

Add optional supporting text:

Sponsors are here to share ideas, solutions, and insights related to your interests.
A short conversation may help you discover new approaches before the conference even begins.

This encourages exploration instead of transactional scheduling.

7. Sponsor Card Interaction Reminder

Above the sponsor cards, add a short line explaining the actions:

Available actions include:
• Schedule an onsite meeting during the conference
• Request an online meeting
• Ask a sponsor to send you more information

This prevents user confusion about button meanings.

8. Layout Consistency with Welcome Page

Ensure the following elements match the Welcome Page:

Element	Requirement
Event logo placement	same alignment
Topic pills	identical style
Section spacing	consistent vertical rhythm
Sponsor card design	unchanged
Header typography	consistent

The goal is visual continuity.

9. Improve Page Intent

The page should clearly communicate:

Purpose:

Discover sponsors aligned with your interests and schedule valuable conversations.

Instead of feeling like a booking utility, it should feel like a curated networking opportunity.

10. Acceptance Criteria

Implementation is complete when:

scheduling page visually matches welcome page structure

topic selection appears prominently

page clearly explains sponsor interaction options

new motivational text replaces the current generic copy

sponsor cards remain unchanged

users arriving from email links immediately understand what to do.
