Refine Messaging Across Cards 1–4 to Better “Sell” the Concierge Experience

These are pure UX copy improvements. No structural changes, just better positioning and language to reinforce value.

1. Card 1 – Remove or Simplify “What Happens Next”
Problem

The “What Happens Next” box is:

redundant

generic

not persuasive

It reads like documentation, not a guided experience.

Required Change
Option A (Recommended)

Remove the “What Happens Next” box entirely

This screen should focus on:

selecting interests

why it matters (already improved earlier)

No need to explain the full process upfront.

Option B (If you want to keep it)

Rewrite it to be tighter and more benefit-driven:

What this unlocks:

• Personalized session recommendations  
• Relevant sponsor connections  
• A smarter event plan tailored to you  

But again, removal is preferred for cleaner UX.

2. Card 2 – Update Summary Metrics to Be Benefit-Driven
Problem

Current labels:

“6 Sessions Available”

“2 Sponsors Matched”

These are neutral and passive.

They don’t communicate value or urgency.

Required Change

Update the metric labels:

Replace:
6 Sessions Available
With:
6 Sessions You Shouldn’t Miss
Replace:
2 Sponsors Matched
With:
2 Sponsors That Match Your Interests
Implementation Notes

Keep numbers dynamic

Only change the label text

Maintain existing styling

3. Card 3 – Rename Section Title
Problem

“Recommended Sessions” is generic and overused.

Required Change
Replace:
Recommended Sessions
With:
Sessions You Don’t Want to Miss
Supporting Copy (Optional Improvement)

If there is subtext under the title, update to:

Based on your interests, these sessions stand out as the most relevant for you.
4. Card 4 – Improve Section Title (Sponsors)
Problem

“Sponsors Matching Your Interests” is functional, but not compelling.

Required Change
Replace:
Sponsors Matching Your Interests
With:
Meet Sponsors That Align With Your Interests
Optional Supporting Copy (Recommended)

Add a short line under the header:

Connect with solution providers that are directly relevant to your priorities.
5. Keep Tone Consistent Across All Cards

Ensure all updated copy follows the same tone:

outcome-focused

benefit-driven

executive-level language

avoids generic terms like “available” or “matched”

You are not describing data — you are guiding decisions.

6. Acceptance Criteria

This update is complete when:

Card 1 no longer includes the generic “What Happens Next” box (or it is rewritten to be concise and benefit-driven)

Card 2 metrics read:

“X Sessions You Shouldn’t Miss”

“X Sponsors That Match Your Interests”

Card 3 header reads:

“Sessions You Don’t Want to Miss”

Card 4 header reads:

“Meet Sponsors That Align With Your Interests”

All changes are text-only and do not break layout or styling

7. Expected Outcome

After these changes:

the flow feels more like a guided, high-value experience

each screen reinforces why the user should care

language aligns with executive-level positioning

the Concierge feels less like a tool and more like a decision assistant

This is a subtle change, but it significantly improves perceived value.
