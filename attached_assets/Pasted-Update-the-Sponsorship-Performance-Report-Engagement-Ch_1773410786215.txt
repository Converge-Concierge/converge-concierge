Update the Sponsorship Performance Report Engagement Chart
Make meetings and information requests the visual priority

We need to revise the new Sponsor Engagement Funnel bar chart in the Sponsorship Performance Report.

Problem

The current chart visually emphasizes:

Profile Views

Meeting CTA Clicks

more than:

Information Requests

Meetings Scheduled

Even though the most valuable outcomes to the sponsor are:

Meetings

Information Requests

Right now the presentation makes those high-value outcomes look small and secondary, which sends the wrong message in the sponsor report.

The chart should reflect business value, not just raw volume.

Primary Goal

Redesign the engagement chart so that:

meetings and information requests are the visual focus

low-value activity like clicks/views is de-emphasized

the report tells a stronger sponsor ROI story

sponsors clearly understand the most important engagement outcomes

1. Do Not Use a Pure Raw-Volume Funnel for This Section
Current issue

The current funnel treats all steps equally and scales the bar sizes only by raw count.

That causes:

Profile Views = 7

Meeting CTA Clicks = 7

to dominate visually, while:

Information Requests = 2

Meetings Scheduled = 2

look minor.

This is misleading in a sponsor-facing report because those smaller numbers are actually the more valuable outcomes.

Required change

Do not present this as a standard raw-volume funnel if that makes the higher-value outcomes look weak.

2. Reframe the Chart Around Sponsor Value

Rename and redesign this section so it reflects engagement value, not just traffic progression.

Preferred new chart title

Use one of these:

High-Value Sponsor Engagement

or

Sponsor Engagement Outcomes

Preferred:

Sponsor Engagement Outcomes

This better matches what matters to the sponsor.

3. Make Meetings and Information Requests the First Items Shown

Reorder the chart so the highest-value outcomes appear first.

New order

Meetings Scheduled

Information Requests

Meeting CTA Clicks

Profile Views

This ensures the sponsor sees the most important outcomes first.

Do not lead with Profile Views.

4. Visually Emphasize High-Value Outcomes

Use visual styling to make the most important outcomes stand out.

Required behavior

Apply stronger styling to:

Meetings Scheduled

Information Requests

Apply lighter/de-emphasized styling to:

Meeting CTA Clicks

Profile Views

Example approach

Meetings Scheduled and Information Requests use the primary event color

Meeting CTA Clicks and Profile Views use lighter neutral or muted secondary styling

This ensures the chart communicates value hierarchy, not just quantity.

5. Do Not Let Low-Value Metrics Dominate the Bar Length Story

If raw counts make low-value items look disproportionately important, adjust the visualization approach.

Acceptable alternatives

Instead of one single same-scale bar chart, use one of these:

Option A — Split the section into two groups
Outcomes

Meetings Scheduled

Information Requests

Interest Signals

Meeting CTA Clicks

Profile Views

This is strongly recommended.

Option B — Use a weighted emphasis chart

Keep all four metrics, but visually highlight the high-value outcomes with:

darker color

bolder labels

callout styling

first position

and de-emphasize clicks/views.

Option C — Use KPI cards for Meetings + Info Requests, and a smaller secondary chart for views/clicks

This is also a good option.

Example:

High-Value Outcomes

Meetings Scheduled: 2

Information Requests: 2

Interest Signals

CTA Clicks: 7

Profile Views: 7

This may be the clearest presentation.

6. Recommended Final Structure

Use this layout:

Section title
Sponsor Engagement Outcomes
A. Primary outcome row or cards

Show prominently:

Meetings Scheduled

Information Requests

B. Secondary interest chart

Show below in smaller or lighter styling:

Meeting CTA Clicks

Profile Views

This makes the report much more sponsor-friendly.

7. Add Short Explanatory Text if Helpful

If useful, add a small line under the section title:

The most meaningful sponsor engagement signals are meetings scheduled and information requests from attendees.

Keep this short and professional.

8. Preserve the Data, But Change the Storytelling

Do not remove Profile Views or Meeting CTA Clicks entirely.

They are still useful as supporting indicators.

But they should be clearly framed as:

early engagement

interest signals

supporting activity

not the main result.

The main result is:

meetings

information requests

9. Use Clear Labels

Update labels if needed so they are easier for sponsors to understand.

Recommended labels:

Meetings Scheduled

Information Requests

Profile Page Views

Meeting Button Clicks

These are slightly more natural than “Meeting CTA Clicks.”

10. Keep It PDF-Friendly

Whatever redesign is used, it must still:

render cleanly in PDF

look polished when printed

avoid clutter

remain readable with small sponsor datasets

Do not overcomplicate the chart.

11. Recommended Preferred Implementation

Preferred implementation for this report:

Section: Sponsor Engagement Outcomes
Primary KPI cards

Meetings Scheduled

Information Requests

Secondary mini bar chart

Profile Page Views

Meeting Button Clicks

This is the clearest and most sponsor-appropriate solution.

12. Acceptance Criteria

This update is complete when:

meetings and information requests are visually emphasized over clicks/views

the chart/section no longer makes low-value metrics look more important than high-value outcomes

the section title and ordering better reflect sponsor value

the report tells a stronger ROI story

13. Success Standard

After this update, a sponsor reading the report should immediately understand that the most important outcomes are:

meetings generated

information requests received

while profile views and clicks are supporting signals only.

That is the standard this chart redesign should meet.
