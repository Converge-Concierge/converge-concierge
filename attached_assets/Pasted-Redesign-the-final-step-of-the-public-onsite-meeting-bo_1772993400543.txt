Redesign the final step of the public onsite meeting booking flow to match the card-based UX used in earlier steps.

Important rules:
- Do not redesign the homepage, event page, sponsor cards, or admin shell
- Do not break existing scheduling logic, legal acknowledgment, confirmation screens, sponsor dashboards, or reports
- Preserve all current working functionality
- Make this a UX/layout improvement using the existing data model where possible

Current problem:
The first three booking steps are simple card-based selections:
- Sponsor
- Date
- Time

But the final step becomes a long form, which breaks the UX pattern and creates friction.

Goal:
Split the final step into 2 short card-based steps that stay as compact and above-the-fold as possible.

==================================================
1. NEW BOOKING FLOW STRUCTURE
==================================================

Change the public onsite booking flow from:

Sponsor → Date → Time → Your Details

to:

Sponsor → Date → Time → Your Info → Confirm

Update the progress indicator accordingly.

==================================================
2. STEP 4: YOUR INFO CARD
==================================================

Replace the current long details form with a compact attendee info card.

Show only the required fields first:
- First Name
- Last Name
- Company
- Title
- Email

Move LinkedIn Profile into an optional/collapsible section:
- Optional Details
  - LinkedIn Profile

Layout requirements:
- Keep the card compact
- Use a 2-column layout on desktop where appropriate
- Stack cleanly on mobile
- Keep the card visually consistent with the previous step cards
- Avoid long scrolling

At the bottom of the card:
- Back button
- Continue button

Behavior:
- Validate required fields before continuing
- Preserve entered values if the user goes back
- Keep existing attendee save/link logic intact

==================================================
3. STEP 5: CONFIRM CARD
==================================================

Add a new final confirmation card after the attendee info step.

This card should summarize:
- Sponsor
- Date
- Time
- Location
- Attendee Name
- Company
- Title
- Email

If LinkedIn was entered, show it optionally/subtly.

At the bottom of the confirm card:
- legal acknowledgment / checkbox
- Back button
- Confirm Meeting button

Legal text:
By scheduling a meeting, you agree to the Terms of Use and Privacy Policy.

Requirements:
- Terms of Use and Privacy Policy remain clickable
- If checkbox is used, require it before final confirmation
- Keep the card compact and visually polished

==================================================
4. ABOVE-THE-FOLD / COMPACT UX
==================================================

Design both new steps so they fit above the fold as much as reasonably possible on desktop.

Do not create another long scrolling page.

Goals:
- preserve the fast, card-based feel
- reduce user friction before confirmation
- make the booking flow feel consistent from start to finish

==================================================
5. MOBILE UX
==================================================

Ensure the new Your Info and Confirm steps work well on mobile:
- stacked layout
- comfortable spacing
- no awkward overflow
- clear primary CTA at the bottom

==================================================
6. RETURNING ATTENDEE QUALITY-OF-LIFE IMPROVEMENT
==================================================

If practical, support a returning attendee experience:

When the entered email matches an existing attendee for that event:
- prefill known attendee details if available
- allow the user to confirm/edit them before continuing

Do not block this task if it is too large, but implement if easy.

==================================================
7. DO NOT CHANGE
==================================================

Do not change:
- sponsor selection step
- date selection step
- time selection step
- underlying meeting record structure unless necessary
- confirmation page after successful booking
- sponsor portal
- admin workflows

==================================================
8. SUCCESS CRITERIA
==================================================

After implementation:
- the booking flow becomes:
  Sponsor → Date → Time → Your Info → Confirm
- the long form is replaced by a compact attendee info card
- there is a separate confirmation summary card
- the flow feels consistent with the earlier card-based UX
- required fields validate correctly
- legal acknowledgment still works
- meeting booking still saves successfully
